Netizen

There was a time when the internet was a city out of sight. A place you couldn't travel to.

A city built by hand; each with a corner that belonged to themself.


Their obsessions spilled through the screen. From the band they loved, the conspiracy they half believed, the poetry they wrote at 3am.
All under a pseudonym that felt more real than their legal name.


There was a charm to the chaos. Often fragments of one's consiousness. Strange but sincere.


The internet used to be a place. Where being online meant being somewhere, instead of on something.

- Essian
